Resources

The Central Post is committed to using its public voice to support social justice initiatives around the world. Listed below are some of the organizations that provide crucial support for those in need and work to achieve a more equitable and just society. If you have the time, we implore you to learn more about these organizations and the causes that they support.

ACLU

The American Civil Liberties Union, or ACLU, is committed to breaking down barriers and providing civil liberties to all groups through the legal system. The group takes on prevalent issues such as immigration, voting rights, and racial justice. The ACLU is the largest public interest law firm in the country, with offices in fifty states. Notably, the group represented plaintiffs in cases such as Obergefell v. Hodges, which affirmed the constitutional right for same-sex couples to marry.

Innocence Project

The Innocence Project uses DNA evidence to exonerate those who are wrongfully convicted. Additionally, the organization works to reform policies that perpetuate wrongful convictions, supports exonerated individuals as they work to acclimate to daily life, and provides statistics, data sources, and literature to help educate researchers and lawyers. To date, the Innocence Project has helped to exonerate hundreds of wrongfully convicted individuals.

Equal Justice Initiative

The Equal Justice Initiative provides legal support for those who are wrongfully convicted, unfairly sentenced, or abused in state jails or prisons. The organization works to alleviate economic and racial injustice by working with marginalized, and often discouraged, groups. Recently, The Equal Justice Initiative has opened the Legacy Museum and the National Memorial for Peace and Justice to address the legacy of racial inequity in the United States.

Human Rights Watch

The Human Rights Watch investigates and brings attention to violations of human rights across the world. The organization pressures governments, armed groups, and businesses to change their laws or practices through advocacy. The Human Rights Watch has accomplished initiatives such as international worker protection, the recognition of the Tulsa Massacre in the United States, and stopping the sale of arms to Saudi Arabia by the United Kingdom due to Yemeni War violations.
